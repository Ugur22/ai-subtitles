## Design Context

### Users
General audience — the app is a personal/side-project tool that's also usable by journalists, podcast producers, and content creators. Users are task-oriented: they arrive with a file, want it transcribed fast, then want to extract value (search, chat, summaries). They're not here to admire the UI — they want it to get out of their way and perform.

### Brand Personality
**Precise. Fast. Clever.**
The interface should feel like a sharp instrument, not a consumer app. It should communicate competence immediately. No fluff, no marketing-speak in the UI, no decorative elements that don't earn their place.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Reference**: Linear — high information density, surgical typography hierarchy, dark neutrals with a single restrained accent, subtle borders that define space without creating visual noise, zero decorative gradients, fast and snappy interactions
- **Theme**: Dark mode only
- **Anti-references**: The current design (indigo-to-purple gradients, Inter font, white card grid, gradient text) — this is the AI slop to escape. Also avoid: glassmorphism, cyan-on-dark, glowing accents, neon, anything that looks like a 2024 SaaS landing page
- **Color direction**: Deep near-black neutrals (not pure black). A single, distinctive accent — not blue/purple/cyan. Consider cool amber, electric emerald, or a very specific electric slate-blue that doesn't read as "AI startup". Tinted neutrals toward the accent hue for subconscious cohesion
- **Typography**: NOT Inter. Use something with character — Geist Sans, DM Sans, or Instrument Sans. Sharp hierarchy: large weight jumps between heading levels, generous letter-spacing on labels, compact body copy

### Design Principles
1. **Density without clutter** — Pack information tightly like a pro tool, not a consumer app. Every pixel should justify its existence.
2. **One accent, used surgically** — Color communicates state and action, not brand. The accent appears only where the user needs to look next.
3. **Speed as a design value** — Transitions are instant or under 150ms. No "delightful" animations that slow down power users. Optimistic UI everywhere.
4. **Flat over layered** — Sections are defined by spacing and subtle borders, not card shadows and nested containers. Avoid stacking white boxes inside gray boxes inside white boxes.
5. **Copy earns its place** — Labels are short and unambiguous. No marketing language inside the app. No emoji in UI chrome.
